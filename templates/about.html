Classical Nahuatl Spelling Normalizer

About the ACK Orthography

The normalizer outputs the normalized phrase in the ACK (Andrews-Campbell-Karttunen) spelling system. This is a spelling system devised by its eponymous scholars: J. Richard Andrews, R.Joseph Campbell, and Frances Karttunen.

It is based off the Classical Nahuatl spelling system. Classical Nahuatl was written at a time before spelling was standardized; ACK provides a more standardized spelling. Writers did not write the glottal fricative (the "h" sound) at the end of syllables, but ACK adds the letter representing the sound at the expected places.
